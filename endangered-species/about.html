The African Wild Dog

An Endangered Species

African Wild Dogs are incredible creatures. They are highly intelligent, and like most canines, they are highly social. They have long, slender legs, a skinny body, and huge, bowl shaped ears. As their latin name - Lycaon pictus, or painted dog - suggests, each one has a uniquely patterned spotted coat, colored tan and black, which helps them blend into the landscape while hunting. Its also helps them recognize each other, even from great distances. When hunting, they stick together with their packs, which are made up of 10 dogs on average, but can grow to up to 40 dogs in some instances. If they ever have fewer than six members, they cannot hunt successfully. African Wild Dogs thrive in arid regions of the Africa Savanna. Their prey consists of such as medium-sized animals such as antelopes, warthogs, wildebeests calves (which are less intimidating in size), and smaller sized animals like rats and birds.
