Venus

The Morning and Evening Star

Venus is the prettiest planet in the galaxy. It has to be, because it is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty. It's also literally the hottest planet in the galaxy. And, after our moon, it is the brightest natural object in the night sky. We can see it sometimes because it is the closest planet to Earth. In fact, Venus is often called Earth's sister planet because of their similar size, gravity, and composition, and the two are classified as terrestrial planets. And, much like sisters, the two are also radically different, in that Venus' atmosphere is 96% carbon dioxide, and its atmospheric pressure is 92 times that of Earth's.

Age: 4.5 billion years

Surface tempurature: 863°F

Size compared to Earth: Just about the same size
