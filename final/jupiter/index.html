Jupiter

The Giant Planet

Ah yes, Jupiter. The largest planet in the Solar System - at lease twice as massive as all other planets combined. Jupiter is so big that it's towing 63 moons around with it. The ultimate sugar daddy. It is also the fastest planet - not to orbit the Sun, but to rotate on its axis. It actually spins so fast that it sticks out a little at the equator, making it less of a perfect circle and more of an oval,. It is one of the gas giants, along with Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune, and it is the third brightest object in the night sky. Jupiter's most unique feature is its Great Red Spot, which is actually a giant storm that has been raging since at least the 17th century, when it was first seen through a telescope. The Spot is three times the diameter of Earth, and is made up of small amounts of sulfur and phosphorus in the ammonia crystals in Jupiter's clouds. The Spot grows and shrinks over time, and shows no time of disppearing any time soon.

Age: 4.5 billion years

Surface tempurature: 234°F
